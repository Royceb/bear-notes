Weed Butter Recipe


Ingredients

  1. 1 cup of butter
  2. 7-10 grams of weed
  3. A mason jar (16 oz is good, but you can use any mason jar)
  4. A cheesecloth
  5. A deep/semi deep pot
  6. An oven
  7. A funnel


1. Decarb (Decarboxylation) the cannabis.


Decarbing just means heating up the weed to a point where the THC becomes active. When we smoke, lighting the weed with fire is basically decarb’ing the weed. When making edibles, you ALWAYS decarb the weed before infusing or mixing it with anything edible. If you don’t the edible will not have much active THC in it, so it won’t get you high.


How to Decarb?


Preheat your oven to 245ºF. Cover a tray with parchment paper to prevent sticking. Place cannabis buds on the tray. You can grind up the weed before this step or after this step. I usually grind it all up before decarb’ing it but I’ve heard of people doing it after and still having good results. Insert the tray into the oven and set a timer for 30-40 minutes. Older, drier cannabis may require less time. Every 10 minutes, gently mix the buds with a light shake of the tray to expose the surface area of the buds equally.


How much weed?


A good ratio for something that won’t knock you out is 7-10 grams of ground decarb’ed weed per 1 cup of butter. Something on the stronger side would have 11-24 grams of weed per 1 cup of butter. For something very light, 3-7 grams per 1 cup is a ok, but I wouldn’t go below 3 grams of weed.


2. Melt Butter


I usually use the highest quality of unsalted butter that I can find, but any butter is fine. Melt one cup of butter and 1 cup of water in a pot. The water helps the butter not burn. Most of the water will completely evaporate by the time we are done. Heat the pot enough so the butter melts, but do not let it come to a boil.


3. Add the weed


As the butter begins to melt, add in the ground, decarb’ed cannabis.


4. Simmer


Maintain low heat (ideally above 160ºF but never exceeding 200ºF) and let the mixture simmer for 2 to 3 hours, stirring occasionally. The mixture should never come to a full boil.


5. Strain

Set a funnel on top of a jar and line it with cheesecloth. Once the butter has cooled off, pour it over the cheesecloth funnel and allow it to strain freely and naturally. Try not to squeeze the cheesecloth yet because it may push more bad-tasting plant material through.


Using a cheesecloth here is very important. There are other things like strainers, but they allow too much small plant particles to seep through. A cheesecloth will make sure you strain all of the butter out without including any particles. It is ok to squeeze the cheesecloth once or twice after the natural strain process finishes to get a few more drops.


6. Refrigerate


Put the jar of butter in the fridge. Check it after 2 or 3 hours. If excess water forms at the bottom of the jar, you can remove the solid butter with a knife and drain the water out. The butter will need to refrigerate for about an hour before removing the water. Once the butter is solid, it is ready to consume!
